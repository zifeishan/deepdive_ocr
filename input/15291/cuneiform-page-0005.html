The sediment is then expelled through the anus (periproct). Most of the differences between an irregular and regular echinoid relate to these differences in mode of feeding.

The posterior migration of the periproct made it possible for the irregular echinoid to leave this large volume of discharged sediment in its trail rather than over its dorsal surface. At the same time that the periproct migrated, most irregular echinoids increased the oxygen-gathering capability of their dorsal tube-feet by greatly broadening them. The result was the formation of the 'petals' so typical of most irregulars. Specialized tube-feet were also produced around the mouth. They were larger, more numerous, and were used to collect sediment that was then passed to the mouth.
